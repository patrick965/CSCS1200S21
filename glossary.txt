Computer: A programmable machine that executes given instructions

Digital: Information stored, often using the binary system, that can be read by a machine

Analog: Continuous transmission of a signal, read by human senses

Electricity: Phenomena associated with the motion of charged particles

Mechanics: Science of machinery 

Hydraulics: Science of liquid in motion

First Hydraulic Computer (MONIAC): The Monetary National Income Analogue Computer was an analog computer that used fluid logic to model an economy

First Mechanical Computer (Pascal's calculator): While it's hard to pinpoint the first mechanical computer, Pascal's calculator is a mechanical calculator that can add or subtract two numbers created in the 17th century 

First electronic computer (ENIAC): Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer, was the first programmable digital computer able to solve numerical problems through reprogramming

Logic: Science of the principles of reasoning

Inference: Something that is derived from reasoning

Semiotics: Study of signs, where sign is anything that communicates meaning that is not the sign itself

Boolean: Data type that has one of two possible values, usually true or false

Binary: Of or relating to the base two number system, where each decimal place corresponds to a power of 2

Black Box: A complicated and mysterious object/device that takes input from a user and turns it into output, usually without the user knowing how
